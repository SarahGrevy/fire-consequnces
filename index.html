Homes in Northern California Face Biggest Fire Consequences

Homes in the northern part of California are more likely to experience severe fire consequences if a fire should occur there relative to other areas in the state. This is according to data from Wildfire Risk to Communities which incorporates the general consequences of fire on a home as a function of fire intensity, using flame length probabilities from wildfire modeling. Homes in northern counties such as Siskiyou and Modoc face more than five times worse consequences should the location catch on fire as compared to southern in-land counties.

In contrast, homes in counties such as San Joaquin and Fresno have a relatively low fire consequence risk, meaning that homes in these areas are more likely to be less damaged should a fire occur. This variation in fire consequences around the state can affect the housing prices since prospective homeowners will stay vary of high-risk fire homes.
